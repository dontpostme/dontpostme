Please don’t post me.

Posting photos and other personal information online can have unintended consequences. If you saw this site on a t-shirt, hat, or elsewhere in the real world. Please respect the privacy of the person who sent you here, and don’t post photos or videos of them on social networks, chat apps or elsewhere on the Internet.

We don’t mean to be antisocial or difficult. Just cautious. Once a photo is online, it is often impossible to erase it. Below you’ll find a few examples of why we’d rather not have our faces online. Please take a look.

For more information take a look at the FAQ.

Unintended Consequences of Over-Sharing

Russian app recognises faces with 70% accuracy

A Russian photographer used a facial recognition app and a social network to identify strangers on the metro with 70% accuracy. It’s going to get more precise.

Tracked everywhere you go

Advertisers and stores are integrating facial recognition into billboards, and malls, and drones to track and profile customers. Like with online ads, your profile can be correlated and tracked without limits.

FAQ

Why are you doing this?

Privacy is important, but it is quickly disappearing in our society. Unfortunately most of us are in the habit of posting everything online and don’t realise that it affects other people. We are doing this to try and help people realise the consequences of over-sharing.

Won’t this just draw extra attention?

It might, yes. But being posted on social media is virtually unavoidable at this point. In many cases this is simply because people are unaware that sharing information about people without their permission is harmful; by making people aware of the issue, hopefully they’ll begin to think twice about their habits.

Can I get involved?

Absolutely! Drop us a message or pull request on github. Or share the website, wear the logos, and simply talk about online privacy with your friends.
